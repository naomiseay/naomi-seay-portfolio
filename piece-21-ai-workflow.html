🧠 My Philosophy

AI tools don't replace careful judgment — they handle the first draft so I can focus on accuracy, quality, and context. I use them to move faster without cutting corners. Every AI output I use gets reviewed, fact-checked, and refined before it goes out under my name or a client's.

🔄 My Standard AI Workflow

1
Understand the real goal
Before using any AI tool, I clarify what success looks like: audience, tone, format, constraints. Garbage in = garbage out — so context-setting is the most important step.
No shortcuts here
2
Generate a first draft with AI
Use the right tool for the task: Claude for text and reasoning, Canva AI for visuals, ChatGPT for imagery. Give it specific instructions and context — not generic prompts.
Speed phase
3
Review, verify, and refine
I read every output critically. I check facts, adjust tone, correct anything that doesn't match the specific context or audience. AI writes the draft; I make it right.
Quality gate
4
Final human judgment call
Before anything goes out, I ask: does this actually solve the problem? Is it appropriate for the context? Would I be comfortable if the client saw how this was made? If yes — it ships.
Accountability
